Analysis of ChIP-Seq data for RUNX2 in an osteosarcoma cell line

2 Methods

For this project, we selected RUNX2 as our TF of interest, and downloaded ChIP-seq peak data from a human osteogenic sarcoma cell line (SaOS-2) from ReMap2022, a database of TF binding regions based on an extensive re-analysis of published ChIP-seq data for human transcription factors. The data contained a total of 6,452 peaks and corresponds to the GSE76937 dataset. We verified the downloaded file contained the same number of peaks, and calculated the total sum of peak lengths (1,382,173 bases), the mean peak length (214.22 bases) and the median peak length (195 bases).

We then used JASPAR to search for transcription factor binding motifs for RUNX2. We found two motifs, MA0511.1 and MA0511.2 and downloaded both matrices in a single TRANSFAC format file, as RUNX2_reference_motifs.tf. We used various RSAT pipelines to search for these reference motifs, and for other TFBS motifs in vertebrates, as well as MEME-ChIP. We performed negative controls with random genome sequences to verify the validity of our findings, and matrix clustering to compare the different motifs found. Finally, we interpreted the results obtained.

Full details on the methodology and the commands used for the analysis can be found in the supplementary material of this project.
